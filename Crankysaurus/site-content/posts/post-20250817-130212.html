One of my goals for this year is to learn Latin. The only way I can explain this decision is to say that it’s something I have wanted to do since I came across the concept of a classical education. I believe I should have been born a little rich boy and sent off to boarding school to learn Latin and Greek. I think there was an administrative error, though, ‘cos I ended up a girl who had to go to a comprehensive school. There I got to learn German, but Latin, Greek and the like were not options that were available to me. I have carefully cultivated a ginormous grudge about this bureaucratic blunder over the decades, but then I realised that I am an adult and there is nothing stopping me from learning Latin on my own. I ordered a Latin textbook, then went in search of something suitably paper-based in which to record my much-anticipated lists of declensions. I found the perfect notebook almost immediately. Here’s what I was thinking when I bought it: I am an old lady learning an ancient language, so it makes sense to have a notebook with extinct creatures on it. (And skateboards!)

A few weeks later I went to an animatronic dinosaur display (which was fabulous, by the way). In the gift shop on the way out, I acquired one T-Rex who I named Rexie Roller:

Rexie was originally a fun mascot for my Latin lessons. But then he started chomping everything, and before I knew it, he had made himself my official Chief Dinosaur Supervisor.

I could say more, but I think it’s probably better to let him speak for himself.
